The Big Picture

Each dot on this graph represents a school. The x-axis represents the percentage of students who have seen their loan balance decrease and have not defaulted 7-years after graduation. The y-axis represents the percentage of the school's student body that have taken out federal loans. After color-coding by school type, three distinct clusters appear.

What about One/Two-Year Programs?

The percentage of students taking out loans appears to be very small at many public schools. The opposite seems true for private for-profit institutions. In addition, the earning prospects are quite a bit lower for one/two-year schools than four-year programs, especially for public and private non-profit.

How Much are Students Taking out in Loans?

The debt students incur by going to private for-profit schools is pretty similar to public schools, and much lower than private non-profit schools.

What's the Median Repayment Rate?

And yet after 7 years, alumni of private for-profit schools are much less likely to be able to repay their student loans. Students attending private non-profit schools appear to be paying off their loans at higher rates and not defaulting, even though they started with a higher median principal.
